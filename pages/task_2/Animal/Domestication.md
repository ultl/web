# Domestication

Many people keep dogs and cats as companions.
Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of pet ownership for the animals involved and for the community as a whole

**Essay:**

There is no doubt how strong the human-animal bond is; thus, keeping pets as companion animals is not unusual in many households’ lives.
These companionships are vital to solving several human problems.
Besides, to some extent, nurturing pets poses several threats to human health.

The first benefit of nurturing pets is improving physical and physiological human health.
Domesticated animals are believed to help pet owners lower the risks of high blood pressure and heart disease.
Besides, people with higher exposure to companion animals are more likely to release stress and several psychological problems.
For example, social support from some pets, such as dogs and cats, might encourage more social interactions with their owners,  reducing stress and the feelings of being isolated or lonely, especially among children from the age of three to six/ children aged three to six.
Second, companion animals are more likely to maintain their owners’ safety.
For example, guide dogs assist blind or visually impaired people in handling their daily activities, such as crossing the streets and getting rid of vehicle accidents.

Besides, there are several drawbacks to pet owners when nurturing domesticated animals.
First, keeping pets as companions might pose several threats to human health.
For example, some pets might be hosts of harmful pathogens, germs, or viruses that might negatively affect human health.
Second, some people might struggle with the expense of pet ownership when these expenses, such as food bills and maintenance costs, may mount up.

In conclusion, even with the benefits of nurturing pets, there are still possible drawbacks to human health and pet owners' financial status.
